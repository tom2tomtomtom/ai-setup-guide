**TEMPLATE 1: Speed & Data Moat (Startup/Foundation)**

For **early-stage companies (Seed to Series A)** with limited resources but high agility and unique early-stage data.

**When to use:**
- **Specific situation 1**: You have a small, focused team and can make decisions in days, not months.
- **Specific situation 2**: You have a unique, proprietary dataset from early customer interactions or a niche market.
- **Specific situation 3**: Your goal is to achieve product-market fit (PMF) and rapid feature velocity.
- **Cost/Timeline note**: Low initial cost, 6-9 month timeline for establishing a measurable advantage.
- **Risk level note**: Moderate risk; the primary risk is focusing on the wrong AI use case.

**Setup Prompt:**
```
CONTEXT: You are a lean, 15-person B2B SaaS startup that has just raised a $3M seed round. Your core product is a [Specific Product Type, e.g., 'project management tool for remote teams']. Your competitive advantage must be built on speed and a proprietary data moat.
SPECIFIC DATA: Your current customer base is 50 paying clients. You have 6 months of historical usage data, totaling 10,000 unique user interactions and 500 support tickets.
GOALS: Achieve 50% faster feature development and 20% higher customer retention than competitors within 9 months.
ANALYZE: Identify the single highest-leverage AI feature that can be built in 3 months using your existing data, and calculate the projected 9-month ROI.
GENERATE: A 9-month roadmap focused on building a "Speed & Data Moat," including the specific AI feature, the required data preparation steps, and 3 key metrics to track.
```

**Practice Scenario:**
A **15-person FinTech startup** (Series A, $5M funding) offers a niche budgeting app for freelancers. They have **1,000 active users** and a unique dataset of **100,000 categorized transactions** over 12 months. Their competitors offer generic budgeting.
- **Starting state**: 5% monthly churn, 3-week feature development cycle.
- **Goal they're trying to achieve**: Reduce churn to 2% and cut feature development time by 50% to outpace competitors.
- **Step-by-step implementation**:
    1. **Data Prep (Month 1)**: Clean and label the 100,000 transactions for "financial health risk" signals.
    2. **AI Feature Build (Months 2-3)**: Develop an AI model that predicts a user's likelihood of churning in the next 30 days based on their transaction patterns (e.g., a 90% risk score).
    3. **Product Integration (Month 4)**: Integrate the model to trigger a proactive, personalized financial tip (e.g., "You spent $500 more on dining this month; here's a plan to get back on track") to high-risk users.
    4. **Process Improvement (Months 1-9)**: Implement an AI coding assistant (e.g., GitHub Copilot) for all 5 engineers to accelerate feature delivery.
- **Expected results with ACTUAL NUMBERS**:
    - **Churn Reduction**: From 5% to 2% (saving 30 users/month, or 360 users/year).
    - **Feature Velocity**: Development cycle reduced from 3 weeks to 1.5 weeks (shipping 10 more features per year).
    - **Value**: At $10/user/month, the saved users generate **$3,600 in additional MRR** ($43,200 ARR) from the AI feature alone.
- **Impact/outcome**: The startup establishes a "Proactive Financial Health" moat, making their product stickier and their development team 2x faster, creating a gap competitors cannot close without 12+ months of effort.

**Success Metrics:**
- [ ] **Feature Velocity**: Average feature development time reduced to 1.5 weeks or less.
- [ ] **Churn Reduction**: Monthly churn rate is consistently below 2.5%.
- [ ] **Model Accuracy**: AI churn prediction model achieves 85% or higher accuracy (F1 score).
- [ ] **Proactive Intervention Rate**: 80% of high-risk users receive a proactive intervention.
- [ ] **Intervention Efficacy**: 50% of users who receive an intervention do not churn in the next 30 days.
- [ ] **Engineering Efficiency**: AI coding assistant is used in 75% of all code commits.
- [ ] **Data Asset Growth**: The labeled transaction dataset grows by 10% each quarter.
- [ ] **Competitive Lead**: Competitor analysis confirms no direct competitor has a similar proactive feature.

**TEMPLATE 2: Integration & Cost Advantage (Mid-Market/Growth)**

For **mid-market companies ($50M - $500M revenue)** with established processes, significant legacy systems, and a need for organization-wide efficiency.

**When to use:**
- **Specific situation 1**: You have high operational costs and manual processes that are ripe for automation.
- **Specific situation 2**: You have large, siloed datasets (e.g., ERP, CRM, HRIS) that need to be connected.
- **Specific situation 3**: Your goal is to achieve a 50% cost advantage in a core business function (e.g., customer support, supply chain).
- **Cost/Timeline note**: Moderate to high cost, 12-18 month timeline for deep integration and measurable cost savings.
- **Risk level note**: High organizational risk; resistance to change and integration complexity are the main hurdles.

**Setup Prompt:**
```
CONTEXT: You are a $200M revenue manufacturing company with 500 employees. Your primary competitive advantage is your high-quality product, but your operational costs are 15% higher than the industry average. You need to use AI to achieve a massive cost advantage through deep integration.
SPECIFIC DATA: You process 5,000 customer support tickets per month, with an average resolution time of 4 hours and a cost of $25 per ticket. You have 5 years of historical ticket data.
GOALS: Reduce the cost per support ticket by 60% and improve resolution time by 75% within 12 months.
ANALYZE: Determine the optimal AI agent strategy (e.g., RAG, fine-tuning) to automate 70% of Tier 1 support tickets, and calculate the projected 12-month cost savings.
GENERATE: A 12-month "Integration & Cost Advantage" plan, detailing the AI system architecture, the required cross-departmental integration points, and the financial impact on the P&L.
```

**Practice Scenario:**
A **$300M e-commerce retailer** (500 employees) processes **5,000 customer support tickets/month**.
- **Starting state**: Average cost per ticket is **$25**, average resolution time is **4 hours**. Total monthly support cost is **$125,000**.
- **Goal they're trying to achieve**: Reduce the cost per ticket to **$10** and resolution time to **1 hour** by automating 70% of Tier 1 inquiries.
- **Step-by-step implementation**:
    1. **Data Prep (Months 1-3)**: Consolidate 5 years of ticket data and knowledge base articles into a single RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system.
    2. **AI Agent Deployment (Months 4-6)**: Deploy an AI agent to handle 70% of common inquiries (e.g., "Where is my order?", "How do I return?").
    3. **Integration (Months 7-9)**: Integrate the AI agent directly with the ERP and CRM systems to provide real-time order status and initiate returns without human intervention.
    4. **Human Augmentation (Months 10-12)**: Equip human agents with AI-powered co-pilots to reduce the handling time for the remaining 30% of complex tickets.
- **Expected results with ACTUAL NUMBERS**:
    - **Automation**: 3,500 tickets/month (70%) handled by AI at a cost of $1/ticket ($3,500 total).
    - **Human Tickets**: 1,500 tickets/month handled by humans at a reduced cost of $15/ticket ($22,500 total).
    - **Total Monthly Cost**: $3,500 + $22,500 = **$26,000**.
    - **Savings**: $125,000 - $26,000 = **$99,000/month** (or **$1.188 million/year**).
- **Impact/outcome**: The company achieves a massive, structural cost advantage in a non-core function, freeing up capital for product innovation and creating a deep integration moat that is difficult for competitors to replicate.

**Success Metrics:**
- [ ] **Cost Per Ticket**: Consistently below the target of $10.
- [ ] **Resolution Time**: Average resolution time for all tickets is below 1 hour.
- [ ] **Automation Rate**: AI agent handles 70% or more of all incoming tickets.
- [ ] **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)**: CSAT score for AI-handled tickets remains above 90%.
- [ ] **System Integration**: AI agent is fully integrated with CRM and ERP for real-time data access.
- [ ] **Agent Efficiency**: Human agent handling time for complex tickets is reduced by 30%.
- [ ] **Annualized Savings**: Total cost savings exceed $1 million in the first year.
- [ ] **System Uptime**: AI support system maintains 99.9% uptime.
- [ ] **Ticket Deflection**: The number of tickets deflected (resolved before reaching a human) is tracked and optimized.

**TEMPLATE 3: Proprietary Moats & Talent Advantage (Enterprise/Scale)**

For **large enterprises ($1B+ revenue)** with vast, unique data assets, significant R&D budgets, and a need to maintain market leadership.

**When to use:**
- **Specific situation 1**: You possess massive, proprietary datasets that are impossible for competitors to acquire (e.g., decades of operational data).

- **Specific situation 3**: You need to attract and retain the world's best AI talent to maintain a technological lead.
- **Cost/Timeline note**: Very high cost, 18-36 month timeline for building and defending proprietary models.
- **Risk level note**: High investment risk; the primary risk is misallocating resources on a non-strategic model.

**Setup Prompt:**
```
CONTEXT: You are a $5B global logistics and supply chain company. Your competitive advantage is your network, but this is being eroded by new, digitally-native competitors. You must build a proprietary model moat to maintain your 10% profit margin.
SPECIFIC DATA: You have 20 years of global shipping data, including weather, port congestion, and historical pricing, totaling 50 petabytes. Your current logistics model predicts delivery times with 85% accuracy.
GOALS: Increase delivery time prediction accuracy to 98% and reduce fuel/delay costs by 5% within 18 months.
ANALYZE: Design a strategy for building a custom "Predictive Logistics Model" (PLM) using your proprietary data, and calculate the projected 18-month savings from reduced delays and optimized routes.
GENERATE: An 18-month "Proprietary Moat & Talent Advantage" plan, including the custom model's architecture, the talent acquisition strategy, and the legal/IP protection steps required to secure the advantage.
```

**Practice Scenario:**
A **$10B global insurance company** (10,000 employees) has a proprietary dataset of **50 million historical claims** and associated legal outcomes.
- **Starting state**: Their current claims processing model has a **75% accuracy** in predicting the final legal cost of a claim, leading to **$50M in annual over-reserves**.
- **Goal they're trying to achieve**: Increase prediction accuracy to **95%** and reduce over-reserves by **$30M** within 18 months.
- **Step-by-step implementation**:
    1. **Model Development (Months 1-9)**: Hire a dedicated team of 5 data scientists to build a custom, fine-tuned LLM (or specialized model) on the 50 million claims dataset.
    2. **Integration & Testing (Months 10-15)**: Integrate the new "Legal Cost Predictor" (LCP) model into the core claims processing system for real-time decision support.
    3. **IP Protection (Months 1-18)**: File patents and implement strict access controls to protect the proprietary model and data.
    4. **Talent Acquisition**: Position the company as a leader in "AI-Driven Risk Management" to attract top-tier AI researchers.
- **Expected results with ACTUAL NUMBERS**:
    - **Accuracy Improvement**: From 75% to 95% in legal cost prediction.
    - **Over-Reserve Reduction**: A 60% reduction in over-reserves, saving **$30 million** in capital that can be reinvested.
    - **Talent Moat**: Successful hiring of 3 PhD-level AI researchers, establishing a long-term R&D advantage.
- **Impact/outcome**: The company creates a proprietary model moat that is impossible for competitors to replicate without the same 50 million claims dataset, leading to a massive capital efficiency advantage and securing its position as a market leader.

**Success Metrics:**
- [ ] **Model Accuracy**: Custom LCP model achieves 95% or higher prediction accuracy.
- [ ] **Capital Efficiency**: Annual over-reserves are reduced by $30 million or more.
- [ ] **Talent Acquisition**: 3+ PhD-level AI researchers are successfully hired and retained.
- [ ] **IP Protection**: Patent application for the LCP model is filed and pending.
- [ ] **Model Performance**: LCP model outperforms the best generic LLM (e.g., GPT-4.5) on a benchmark claims dataset by 20% or more.
- [ ] **Deployment Rate**: LCP model is fully integrated and used in 100% of all relevant claims.
- [ ] **Executive Buy-in**: Quarterly reports confirm executive alignment on the proprietary model strategy.
- [ ] **Data Security**: Zero unauthorized access incidents to the proprietary claims dataset.
- [ ] **R&D Pipeline**: A roadmap for the next 3 proprietary models is established.
