**TEMPLATE 1: The "Single-Agent Accelerator" (Startup/Foundation)**

For **early-stage startups (Seed to Series A)** focused on automating one high-impact, repetitive workflow to free founder time.

**When to use:**
- **Specific situation 1**: You have a single, well-defined process that consumes 10+ hours/week of founder or team time.
- **Specific situation 2**: The process is rules-based with clear success criteria (e.g., lead qualification, invoice processing).
- **Specific situation 3**: You have limited technical resources and need a quick, high-ROI win.
- **Cost/Timeline note**: Investment is typically $5K - $30K, with a 1-3 month timeline to production.
- **Risk level note**: Low risk (single process), high learning value (foundation for future agents).

**Setup Prompt:**
```
Act as an AI Automation Consultant for a startup. I need to design a single autonomous agent to handle [PROCESS_NAME] end-to-end.

CONTEXT: The company is a [COMPANY_SIZE_AND_STAGE] [INDUSTRY] startup. Currently, [PROCESS_NAME] takes [TIME_PER_INSTANCE] and is done [FREQUENCY] times per [WEEK/MONTH]. This process involves [STEP_1], [STEP_2], and [STEP_3].
SPECIFIC DATA: The desired outcome is [SUCCESS_CRITERIA]. The agent will use [AI_MODEL] and integrate with [EXISTING_TOOLS].
GOALS: The agent must autonomously handle 80%+ of cases, escalating only edge cases to humans.
ANALYZE: Calculate the time saved per week and the annual value of that time at [HOURLY_RATE].
GENERATE: A step-by-step deployment plan, including the agent's decision logic, escalation rules, and success metrics.
```

**Practice Scenario:**
A **Seed-stage SaaS startup** (8 employees, $500K ARR) in the B2B marketing automation space.
**Starting state**: The founder spends 12 hours/week manually qualifying inbound leads from the website contact form. 60% of leads are unqualified (wrong company size, no budget, etc.).
**Goal they're trying to achieve**: Deploy an autonomous agent to qualify leads in real-time, routing only qualified leads (40%) to the sales team.
**Step-by-step implementation**:
1. **Define Rules (Week 1)**: Document the lead qualification criteria (company size > 50 employees, budget > $10K/year, decision-maker title).
2. **Build Agent (Week 2-3)**: Use `gpt-4.1-mini` via API to analyze lead form submissions against criteria.
3. **Test (Week 4)**: Run the agent in parallel with manual qualification for 50 leads to validate accuracy.
4. **Deploy (Week 5)**: Activate the agent to auto-qualify all new leads, with a Slack notification for edge cases.
**Expected results with ACTUAL NUMBERS**:
- **Time Saved**: 12 hours/week → **2 hours/week** (83% reduction, founder now focuses on closing deals).
- **Lead Response Time**: 24 hours → **5 minutes** (480x faster, improving conversion by 15%).
- **Annual Value**: 10 hours/week saved × 50 weeks × $200/hour = **$100,000** in founder time recovered.
**Impact/outcome**: The founder redirects 500 hours/year to product development and fundraising, accelerating the path to Series A.

**Success Metrics:**
- [ ] Agent achieves 90%+ accuracy in lead qualification (validated against manual review).
- [ ] Time spent on lead qualification reduced by 80%+.
- [ ] Lead response time reduced to under 10 minutes.
- [ ] Escalation rate to humans is below 20% of total leads.
- [ ] The agent's cost-per-lead is tracked and remains below $2.
- [ ] Sales team reports higher lead quality (fewer unqualified meetings).
- [ ] The agent runs continuously for 30 days with zero critical failures.
- [ ] Founder time saved is reinvested in high-value activities (tracked).

**TEMPLATE 2: The "Process Swarm" (Mid-Market/Acceleration)**

For **Mid-Market companies ($10M - $100M revenue)** deploying multiple coordinated agents to automate interconnected workflows.

**When to use:**
- **Specific situation 1**: You have 3-5 related processes that currently require handoffs between teams (e.g., order-to-cash, support-to-product).
- **Specific situation 2**: Your goal is to reduce cycle time and eliminate manual handoffs across departments.
- **Specific situation 3**: You have a dedicated operations or IT team to manage agent orchestration.
- **Cost/Timeline note**: Investment is typically $100K - $500K, with a 6-12 month timeline to full deployment.
- **Risk level note**: Moderate risk (cross-functional coordination), high ROI potential (eliminating bottlenecks).

**Setup Prompt:**
```
Act as an AI Operations Architect for a Mid-Market company. I need to design a multi-agent system to automate the [WORKFLOW_NAME] process, which currently spans [NUMBER_OF_TEAMS] teams.

CONTEXT: The company is a [COMPANY_SIZE_AND_STAGE] [INDUSTRY] firm. The [WORKFLOW_NAME] process involves [TEAM_1] doing [TASK_1], then [TEAM_2] doing [TASK_2], and finally [TEAM_3] doing [TASK_3]. The current cycle time is [CURRENT_CYCLE_TIME].
SPECIFIC DATA: Each agent will handle one task autonomously. Agent 1 will [AGENT_1_TASK], Agent 2 will [AGENT_2_TASK], and Agent 3 will [AGENT_3_TASK]. The agents will communicate via [INTEGRATION_METHOD].
GOALS: Reduce the cycle time by 70%+ and eliminate manual handoffs.
ANALYZE: Calculate the cycle time reduction and the annual cost savings from reduced labor and faster throughput.
GENERATE: A multi-agent orchestration plan, including agent responsibilities, communication protocols, and escalation workflows.
```

**Practice Scenario:**
A **Mid-Market e-commerce company** (200 employees, $50M revenue) in the consumer electronics space.
**Starting state**: The "order-to-fulfillment" process takes 48 hours and involves 3 teams: (1) Customer Service validates the order, (2) Warehouse picks and packs, (3) Logistics schedules shipping. Manual handoffs cause delays.
**Goal they're trying to achieve**: Deploy 3 coordinated agents to reduce the cycle time to 6 hours and eliminate all manual handoffs.
**Step-by-step implementation**:
1. **Agent 1 - Order Validation (Month 1-2)**: Autonomously validates payment, inventory, and shipping address; flags edge cases (e.g., international orders).
2. **Agent 2 - Warehouse Automation (Month 3-4)**: Receives validated orders, generates pick lists, and triggers robotic picking systems.
3. **Agent 3 - Logistics Optimization (Month 5-6)**: Selects the optimal carrier based on cost/speed, generates labels, and schedules pickup.
4. **Orchestration (Month 7-8)**: Integrate all agents via a central workflow engine (e.g., Zapier, n8n) with real-time status updates.
**Expected results with ACTUAL NUMBERS**:
- **Cycle Time**: 48 hours → **6 hours** (87.5% reduction).
- **Manual Handoffs**: 3 handoffs → **0 handoffs** (100% elimination).
- **Annual Savings**: 10 FTEs redeployed to customer experience roles = **$800,000** in labor optimization.
- **Throughput**: Order capacity increased by 50% with the same warehouse footprint.
**Impact/outcome**: The company scales to $75M revenue without adding warehouse staff, improving margins by 5%.

**Success Metrics:**
- [ ] Cycle time reduced by 80%+ from baseline.
- [ ] Manual handoffs eliminated (0 human touches for 90%+ of orders).
- [ ] Agent-to-agent communication success rate is 99%+.
- [ ] Error rate (e.g., wrong item shipped) remains below 0.5%.
- [ ] At least 5 FTEs are redeployed to higher-value roles.
- [ ] Customer satisfaction (CSAT) improves by 10%+ due to faster fulfillment.
- [ ] The multi-agent system runs for 90 days with zero critical failures.
- [ ] Total cost of ownership (TCO) for the agent system is 50% lower than the manual process.

**TEMPLATE 3: The "Enterprise Agent Fabric" (Enterprise/Transformation)**

For **Large Enterprises ($500M+ revenue)** deploying organization-wide agent networks to transform core business operations.

**When to use:**
- **Specific situation 1**: You need to automate complex, multi-departmental processes that involve 10+ steps and multiple decision points (e.g., M&A due diligence, regulatory reporting).
- **Specific situation 2**: Your goal is to achieve 10x efficiency gains and enable capabilities that were previously impossible (e.g., real-time compliance monitoring).
- **Specific situation 3**: You have executive sponsorship, a dedicated AI Center of Excellence, and a 12-24 month transformation timeline.
- **Cost/Timeline note**: Investment is typically $2M - $10M+, with an 18-24 month timeline to full-scale deployment.
- **Risk level note**: Low technical risk (proven tech), high organizational risk (requires cultural transformation).

**Setup Prompt:**
```
Act as the Chief AI Officer for a Fortune 500 company. I need to design an enterprise-wide agent fabric to transform the [CORE_BUSINESS_PROCESS] across [NUMBER_OF_BUSINESS_UNITS] business units.

CONTEXT: The company is a [COMPANY_SIZE_AND_STAGE] [INDUSTRY] enterprise. The [CORE_BUSINESS_PROCESS] currently involves [NUMBER_OF_STEPS] steps, [NUMBER_OF_SYSTEMS] legacy systems, and [NUMBER_OF_EMPLOYEES] employees. The process takes [CURRENT_CYCLE_TIME] and has a [ERROR_RATE]% error rate.
SPECIFIC DATA: The agent fabric will consist of [NUMBER_OF_AGENTS] specialized agents, each handling a specific sub-process. The agents will integrate with [SYSTEM_1], [SYSTEM_2], and [SYSTEM_3] via APIs. The total investment is [INVESTMENT_COST].
GOALS: Reduce cycle time by 90%+, eliminate errors, and enable real-time decision-making.
ANALYZE: Calculate the cycle time reduction, error reduction, and the annual value of efficiency gains and risk mitigation.
GENERATE: A phased deployment plan, including governance frameworks, change management strategies, and success metrics for each phase.
```

**Practice Scenario:**
A **Global pharmaceutical company** (15,000 employees, $8B revenue) in the drug development and regulatory approval space.
**Starting state**: The "clinical trial data analysis and regulatory submission" process takes 18 months, involves 50+ employees, and has a 5% error rate in submissions (causing costly delays).
**Goal they're trying to achieve**: Deploy an enterprise agent fabric to reduce the cycle time to 6 months, eliminate submission errors, and enable real-time compliance monitoring.
**Step-by-step implementation**:
1. **Governance (Month 1-6)**: Establish an AI Ethics Board, define agent responsibilities, and secure regulatory pre-approval for AI-assisted submissions.
2. **Agent Development (Month 7-12)**: Build 10 specialized agents (e.g., Data Cleaning Agent, Statistical Analysis Agent, Regulatory Document Generation Agent).
3. **Integration (Month 13-18)**: Integrate agents with clinical trial management systems, data lakes, and regulatory submission portals.
4. **Pilot (Month 19-21)**: Run the agent fabric on one clinical trial in parallel with the manual process to validate accuracy.
5. **Scale (Month 22-24)**: Deploy the agent fabric across all active clinical trials.
**Expected results with ACTUAL NUMBERS**:
- **Cycle Time**: 18 months → **6 months** (66.7% reduction).
- **Submission Error Rate**: 5% → **0.1%** (98% reduction in errors).
- **Annual Value**: Faster time-to-market for 3 drugs = **$500 Million** in additional revenue (each drug generates $150M-200M/year).
- **Compliance**: Real-time monitoring prevents 2 potential regulatory violations, avoiding **$50 Million** in fines.
**Impact/outcome**: The company becomes the industry leader in regulatory efficiency, accelerating drug approvals and improving patient outcomes.

**Success Metrics:**
- [ ] Cycle time reduced by 60%+ from baseline.
- [ ] Submission error rate reduced to below 0.5%.
- [ ] At least 30 FTEs are redeployed to strategic R&D roles.
- [ ] The agent fabric passes an external regulatory audit with zero findings.
- [ ] Real-time compliance monitoring is operational across all trials.
- [ ] The system's explainability (XAI) meets regulatory standards.
- [ ] Total cost of ownership (TCO) is 70% lower than the manual process.
- [ ] The agent fabric is successfully deployed in at least 3 geographical regions.
- [ ] Executive leadership reports the agent fabric as a top-3 strategic initiative.
