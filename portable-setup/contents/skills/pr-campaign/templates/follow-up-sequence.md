# Follow-Up Sequence

Cadence differs sharply between **PR pitches to journalists** and **warm outreach to industry contacts**. Mixing the two cadences is the #1 way solo founders get blocked by journalists.

## PR cadence (journalists, editors, podcast hosts)

Backed by Muck Rack 2025 State of Journalism (n=1,600+) and Cision 2025 State of the Media (n=3,000+).

**The cap is ONE follow-up. Two is the absolute ceiling. Three or more damages the relationship and reduces likelihood of future pickup across all your future pitches.**

| Touch | Day | Channel | Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | The pitch |
| 2 | Day 3-5 | Email (same thread) | One follow-up with *new* information. Stop here. |

Hard rules:
- **62% of journalists want only one follow-up.** Send it once. Then stop.
- **The follow-up MUST add new information.** A new metric, a new endorsement, a second example, a related news peg. A pure "bumping this" is wasted.
- **Open-but-no-reply: switch channel before sending a second email.** If a tracker shows the journalist opened your pitch 2+ times without replying, send a LinkedIn DM, not a follow-up email.
- **No-open after 5 days: walk away.** They didn't see it. Sending again hits the same closed-tab. Wait 60+ days for a genuinely new angle.

## Warm outreach cadence (industry contacts, demo requests, peer pitches)

This is NOT for journalists. This is for: agency CEOs, peer founders, prospective customers, intro requests, demo trial pitches.

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Initial pitch |
| 2 | Day 3 | Email (same thread) | Bump with new angle or proof point |
| 3 | Day 10-14 | LinkedIn DM | Different channel, casual register |
| 4 | Day 21 | Email | Graceful exit |
| 5 | Day 60+ | Email | Re-approach with new asset |

Stop at touch 4. The graceful exit is the highest-converting follow-up across most outbound benchmarks.

## The "no-pitch intro" warming sequence (use 30-60 days before launch)

The single highest-leverage move in modern PR: 85% of journalists explicitly welcome an introductory email with no pitch attached (Cision 2025).

For top-priority journalists, send a one-line warming email 30-60 days before you actually need anything:

> Hey [name],
>
> Read your piece on [specific topic] last week. The [specific paragraph or argument] stuck with me.
>
> I'm building [one-sentence thing]. Not pitching today, just figured I'd land in your inbox once before I have something worth your attention.
>
> Cheers, Tom

That's the entire email. No links. No demo. No ask. The point is name recognition. When the actual pitch arrives 30-60 days later, you're a name they've seen, not a cold name.

## Follow-up copy patterns

### PR follow-up (Touch 2 only, must add new info)

Keep under 60 words. The follow-up should add something material.

Patterns that work:

> Following up. Since I sent this, [new endorsement / new coverage / new use case]. Demo's still live at [link].

> Wanted to flag: [adjacent journalist] picked it up in their newsletter this morning. Curious if it still fits your beat.

> Bumping this with one new data point. [Specific metric or quote]. The demo is at [link] if useful.

NEVER for PR:
- "Just bumping this to the top of your inbox"
- "Checking in"
- "Was wondering if you had a chance"
- "Sorry to bother you again"

### Warm outreach follow-ups (Touches 2-4)

See sections above. More latitude here because the relationship norms are different.

## Channel switching

When an email isn't getting through, the right move is often to change channel. The Muck Rack data shows 85% of journalists prefer email even for ongoing communication, but for follow-ups specifically, a LinkedIn DM or X reply often re-opens a stalled thread without the relationship damage of a third email.

The script:

> Hey, sent you an email last week about [specific topic]. Inbox might be busy. Happy to resend or chat here if easier.

Short. Casual. Acknowledges they might be busy. Offers a low-friction option.

## Best send windows (recipient timezone, not sender timezone)

Aggregated across Muck Rack, Cision, Outreach 2024-2025 data.

| Day | Best window (recipient local) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tuesday | 5am-12pm | Single highest-engagement window |
| Wednesday | 9am-12pm | Strong second |
| Thursday | 9am-3pm | Strong, especially for B2B |
| Tuesday-Thursday | 1-3pm | Second daily peak (post-lunch inbox) |
| Monday | After 10am only | Mondays before 10am are dead |
| Friday | Before 12pm only | Friday afternoons are dead |
| Sat/Sun | Avoid for cold pitches | Hurts engagement even with AI-builder audiences |

**Critical:** Send in *their* timezone, not yours. Melbourne afternoon = US midnight = London 5am. Use scheduled send in Gmail. This single change typically lifts open rates 10-15 points.

For Tom's most common targets:
- **AU trade press** (Mumbrella, Unmade, AdNews, B&T): 8-10am AEST Tuesday
- **US tech press** (Platformer, etc): 7-9am PT Tuesday
- **UK / EU AI community** (Willison, etc): 8-10am GMT Tuesday

## Tracking what's worked

For every campaign, log to the campaign tracker:
- Touch number that converted (for PR, most replies come on touch 1 or touch 2)
- Subject line variant tested
- Day of week / time of day sent (in recipient timezone)
- Channel used (email / LinkedIn / X)
- Reply tone (interested / wants more / passing / dead)

After 3 campaigns, the personal data outweighs the industry benchmarks. Use your own.
