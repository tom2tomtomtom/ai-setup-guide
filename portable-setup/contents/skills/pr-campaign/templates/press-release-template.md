# Press Release Template

Use for trade press multi-outlet sends, formal product announcements, or when an asset is substantial enough that multiple journalists will want the same source document. For single warm pitches, use `pitch-email-template.md` instead.

## When to use a press release

- The asset has a hard news hook (launch, milestone, partnership, funding, named customer signing)
- You're sending to three or more outlets in the same vertical
- The story has quotable lines and verifiable numbers
- The story needs to be published exactly the same way across outlets

## When NOT to use one

- Single-outlet exclusive pitches (those are 1:1 emails, not releases)
- Stories without a hard news peg
- Brand thought leadership (those go in posts, not press)
- Pre-launch teasers (use anticipation drops, not formal releases)

## The structure

One page maximum. 400-500 words. The journalist needs to lift quotes and paragraphs verbatim and have the piece write itself.

### Header

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
or
EMBARGOED UNTIL [DATE TIME TIMEZONE]
```

Default to "FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE" unless coordinating a multi-outlet drop. Embargoes are honoured less in 2024-2026 than they used to be. Only use when you have a major outlet that demanded one.

### Headline (one line, under 90 characters)

State the story. Not the brand. Not the feature. The story.

- Bad: *"Redbaez Launches New AI Demo"*
- Good: *"Melbourne-built AI catches Claude judging Claude as human, in live Fountain Tomato Sauce test"*

The headline should make a journalist think: "I want to read the next sentence."

### Sub-headline (optional, one line)

Used to add the dimension the headline can't carry. Often the verifiable claim or the proof point.

### Dateline (one line)

```
**CITY, DATE.** [Lead paragraph starts here.]
```

For Tom: `**MELBOURNE, 21 May 2026.**`

### Lead paragraph (40-60 words)

The 5 Ws compressed. Who built what, why it matters, what's new. Should be quotable as a single paragraph if the journalist copy-pastes nothing else.

### Body paragraphs (3-4 paragraphs, 60-100 words each)

Order matters. Inverted pyramid: most important first, declining importance after.

1. **The mechanism.** How the thing works. Use the canonical description verbatim, bolded as a standalone paragraph.
2. **The proof.** A specific scene from the work. Names, numbers, quotes. The Fountain transcript moment for Subjectivity.
3. **The pull quote.** A blockquoted line from the work or the founder that is the most shareable.
4. **The founder quote.** One quote attributable to Tom, in his actual voice. Press will lift this verbatim. Make it sound like him, not like a quote.

### Founder quote rules

- 1-2 sentences max
- In Tom's voice: direct, specific, no consultant language, no em-dashes
- Says something that couldn't have come from a press release template
- Attributable: `"...," said Hyde.`

Bad: *"We're excited to be at the forefront of AI innovation, and we believe this represents a new chapter for the industry."*

Good: *"I've been pitching the architecture for months. Now Claude is doing the pitching for me."*

### Closing context (one paragraph)

Bio of founder, prior context, link to verify (live demo, essay, etc).

### Boilerplate

Three short blocks:

```
### About [Product / Company]

[2-3 sentence description.]

### Press contact

[Name]
[Title]
[Email]
[Phone]
[URL]
```

## What to attach

- The full transcript or supporting document as .docx (not PDF, journalists prefer editable for copy-paste)
- High-res screenshot of the most quotable moment
- Founder headshot (low-resolution OK, journalists rarely use them in trade press)
- Link to live verification (demo, essay, product page)

Maximum two attachments. Anything more reads like a media kit and journalists ignore media kits.

## Anti-patterns

- No marketing speak ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "industry-leading", "best-in-class", "synergistic")
- No future tense for things that haven't happened ("will revolutionise", "is set to disrupt")
- No vague numbers ("dozens of customers", "significant growth"). Either give the number or don't mention it.
- No three-paragraph "About" boilerplate. Three sentences max.
- No more than two quoted attributables. The founder, and maybe one customer.
- No PDFs. Plain text email body plus optional .docx attachment.

## Send format

Press releases go in the email body as plain text/HTML, NOT as a PDF attachment. The journalist needs to see it without clicking. The .docx attachment is for the supporting transcript or asset, not the release itself.

Subject line for the email: the headline of the release, optionally prefixed with `[For Immediate Release]` or `[Embargoed: DATE]`.
